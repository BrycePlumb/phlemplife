Is Life Insurance the Solution for You?

Read about these real world cases where life insurance is a solution

You have bills, debts, and dependents to take care of. To protect your family from inheriting these responsibilities, you apply for a life insurance policy that can meet these financial obligations if you pass away.

You are a majority shareholder in a business that is growing. It is important to you and your shareholders for there to be liquidity available if a key person in your business becomes unable to do their duties or passes away. To protect your business from the loss of losing a key person, your business applies for life insurance policies on all of the shareholders and key members of the business.

Your finances are growing as you age, however you are worried that a major medical event could disrupt your financial strategy. You apply for a policy that includes living benefits to ensure that there will be money available in the event of a critical, chronic, or terminal illness.

You've been growing your savings and are starting to wonder if there is a way to earn a higher interest rate on your savings. You apply for a life insurance policy with permanent death benefit protection and your financial professional helps you design a policy with cash value accumulation.

Life Insurance can do more than you expect.

Policy loans from life insurance policies generally are not subject to income tax, provided the contract is not a Modified Endowment Contract (MEC), as defined by Section 7702A of the Internal Revenue Code. A policy loan or withdrawal from a life insurance policy that is a MEC is taxable upon receipt to the extent cash value of the contract exceeds premium paid. Distributions from MECs are subject to federal income tax to the extent of the gain in the policy and taxable distributions are subject to a 10% additional tax prior to age 59½, with certain exceptions. Policy loans and withdrawals will reduce cash value and death benefit. Policy loans are subject to interest charges. Consult with and rely on your tax advisor or attorney on your specific situation. Income and growth on accumulated cash values is generally taxable only upon withdrawal. Adverse tax consequences may result if withdrawals exceed premiums paid into the policy. Withdrawals or surrenders made during a Surrender Charge period will be subject to withdrawal charges, processing fees, or surrender charges, and may reduce the ultimate death benefit and cash value. Surrender charges vary by product, issue age, sex, underwriting class, and policy year.
