Single Premium Immediate Annuity: Buy a Stream of Income

The Single Premium Immediate Annuity (SPIA) is a type of annuity that offers a locked-in interest rate for either a specified period of time or for life. Payments from an SPIA begin immediately after a lump sum payment from a savings or retirement plan is made. There is also a Single Premium Deferred Annuity (SPDA), which allows the annuity owner to defer payments until a later date. Both SPIA and SPDA interest is credited by an insurance company with an interest rate set at the time of purchase.

Fixed Indexed Annuity: Stream of Income Linked to the Changes in an Index

A Fixed Indexed Annuity (FIA) is a type of annuity where the interest rate is linked to the performance of a financial index. With an FIA, there is protection from market losses as any interest credited is not tied to stock market gains. 1 FIA owners can choose to make a lump sum payment or a series of payments and decide whether to defer payments or begin receiving payments immediately. The interest is credited by an insurance company and is not tied to stock market gains, providing additional protection from market losses.2

Multi-Year Guarantee Annuity: Fixed Term Annuity

A Multi-Year Guarantee Annuity (MYGA) is a type of fixed term annuity where the insurance company guarantees to pay a fixed interest rate for a specific number of years, typically 3, 5, or 7 years. After purchasing the annuity with a lump sum payment, the annuity owner starts to receive payments for a specified period of time. MYGAs provide a guaranteed source of income for a set period of time.
