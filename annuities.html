Annuities

Annuities are long-term investments that can provide a steady stream of income in retirement. These are a popular choice for individuals who want to take market risk out of their portfolio and retirement plans.

Types of Annuities

  • Immediate annuities are a type of annuity that begins providing income immediately after purchase. They are also known as "single premium immediate annuities" (SPIA) because they are funded with a single lump-sum payment. Immediate annuities are designed for people who want to convert a lump sum of money into a steady stream of income, usually during their retirement years. This income stream is often used to cover basic expenses like food, housing, and healthcare.
  • Deferred annuities are a type of annuity that allows the annuitant to accumulate value over time before providing income. They are also known as "deferred annuity contracts" or "deferred income annuities" (DIA). Deferred annuities are designed for people who want to save for retirement and convert their savings into a steady stream of income at a later date. They are typically funded with regular contributions over an extended period of time, such as years or decades.
  • Fixed annuities are a type of annuity that provides a fixed rate of return. They are also known as "fixed rate annuities" or "fixed term annuities". Fixed annuities are designed for people who want a guaranteed, stable rate of return on their investment. They are typically funded with a lump sum of money or a series of contributions, and the insurance company guarantees a fixed rate of return for a specific period of time, such as one, five or ten years. The fixed rate of return on a fixed annuity is determined by the insurance company, and it is usually based on the prevailing interest rates at the time of purchase. The rate of return is guaranteed and will not change, regardless of market fluctuations.
  • Indexed annuities are a type of annuity that provide a rate of return that is linked to the performance of an index, such as the S&P 500. Indexed annuities are for people who want the potential for higher returns than fixed annuities, while also protecting their principal investment from market fluctuations. An insurance company guarantees a minimum rate of return, while also offering the potential for higher returns based on the performance of the index.
  • When purchasing an annuity, the annuitant chooses the payment frequency, the accumulation period and the payout option. The most common payout options for annuties are: Lifetime income: The annuity pays income for the lifetime of the annuitant, regardless of how long they live. Joint and Survivor: The annuity pays income for the lifetime of the annuitant and a joint annuitant (such as a spouse), and the income continues to be paid to the survivor after the first person dies. Term Certain: The annuity pays income for a fixed number of years, regardless of how long the annuitant lives. Fixed annuities can provide a guaranteed rate of return and can protect the annuitant's principal investment from market fluctuations. Additionally, they can provide a guaranteed income stream in the future which can help to protect against the risk of outliving one's savings. However, annuities also come with trade-offs. The annuitant may be subject to surrender charges if they withdraw funds early. It's important to consider the long-term financial goals and the retirement plan before purchasing an annuity, and to consult with a financial professional to understand the terms and conditions, fees, and risks associated with the annuity.
