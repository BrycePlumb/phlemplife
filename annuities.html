SPIA

The single premium immediate annuity (SPIA) has a locked in interest rate for life or a certain period of time. Pyaments from an SPIA begin immediately after the annuity is purchased with a lump sum amount from a savings or retirment plan. There is also a Single Premium Deferred Annuity (SPDA), that allows the owner of the annuity to defer the payments they will receive until a later date. The interest on both types of annuities is credited by an insurance company with an interest rate set at the time of purchase.

FIA

The interest rate of a fixed indexed annuity (FIA) is tied to the changes in an index. There is protection from market losses because the interest credited is not tied to stock market gains.1 The annuity owner can pay with a lump sum or series of payments and they also choose whether to defer the payments until a later date or begin receiving payments immediately. There is protection from market losses because the interest credited is not tied to stock market gains and is credited by an insurance company.2

MYGA

For a multi year guarantee annuity (MYGA) the insurance company pays a guaranteed interest rate for a specific number of years, usually 3, 5, or 7 years. The annuity owner starts to receive payments for a specific period of time after purchasing the annuity with a lump sum payment.
